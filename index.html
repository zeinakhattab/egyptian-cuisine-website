Egyptian Cuisine opened in 2014. Chef / Owner Zeina Khattab began by making her cultural food and selling them to restaurants and her Irish neighbors out of a small kitchen. Today, Ireland's beloved Egyptian restaurant celebrates 8 years of classic, made from scratch Egyptian cooking.

Our Specials

Kushari

Rice, macaroni and lentils, topped with tomato sauce, garlic vinegar and garnished with chickpeas and fried onions.

Ful wa Ta’meya

The ful is made of fava beans that are cooked for several hours and seasoned and served with hummus. The ta-meya, which is an Egyptian falafel, is made out of crushed fava beans that is later made into a paste, then fried.

Fiteer Baladi

Fiteer is made of plenty of filo pastry layers cooked in a brick oven. Can beserved plain, savoury with meats, cheese and vegetables or sweet with syrup, honey or sugar.

Chicken Shawarma

Layers of meat which could be lamb, chicken or beef that are placed on a spit and grilled. The outer layers are then shaved off and chucked into a tasty hunk of bread or roll.

Kofta and Kebab

Kofta is minced beef or lamb seasoned with plenty of spices, which is then rolled and grilled. Kebab is prepped with pieces meat such as lamb or veal that are grilled over a coal fire on a skewer. These meat dishes are served with sides of dips, rice, breads and veges.
